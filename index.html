Discovery Call Framework
Questions to ask in order — copy to your notes before every call
Phase 1 — Understand the current state
01
Walk me through how your team handles [process] today, step by step.
02
How many people touch this process every week, and how much time does it take them?
03
What tools are you using right now — Excel, email, paper? What's broken about it?
04
What happens when something falls through the cracks? Can you give me an example?
Phase 2 — Quantify the pain
05
If I could eliminate this problem entirely, what would that free up for your team?
06
Has this caused a mistake that cost you money or a client? What was that worth?
07
Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?
Phase 3 — Qualify budget and timeline
08
Is solving this a priority this quarter, or is it more of a nice-to-have for later?
09
Have you budgeted for this kind of project? Roughly what range were you thinking?
10
Who else needs to be involved in the decision — is it just you?
Phase 4 — Close the call
11
Based on what you've told me, I think I can build exactly what you need. Would it make sense for me to send you a proposal this week?
12
If the proposal looks good, what would the next step look like on your end?
Objection Responses
Click an objection to see exactly what to say
"It's too expensive."
I understand — price is always a consideration. Let me ask: if this system saves your team 10 hours per week, what's that worth over the next year? Most clients find the system pays for itself within 2–3 months. Would it help if I showed you how I arrived at this number?
"We'll build it internally."
Absolutely — and many companies try that. The challenge is that internal teams are usually pulled between this project and their actual job. I can deliver a working system in under two weeks. What's a realistic timeline if your team takes it on themselves?
"Not the right time."
I hear that a lot. Can I ask — what needs to happen for this to become a priority? If it's a Q3 budget issue, I can hold a proposal and follow up then. I'd rather work with your timeline than push for something that doesn't fit.
"Can you do it for cheaper?"
I can look at reducing scope — for example, we could phase the project and start with the core module only. That said, I'd rather not cut corners on something your team will use every day. What part of the system is most critical to you?
"We already have software for that."
Good to know — what are you using? The issue with most generic software is that it's built for everyone, which means it fits no one perfectly. What I build is designed specifically around your workflow. Is your current tool covering everything you need, or are there still gaps?
"Send me some information and I'll look at it."
Of course. Before I send anything — is there a specific part you want me to focus on, or a concern I haven't addressed yet? I want to make sure what I send is actually useful, not just a generic deck. And when should I follow up after you've had a chance to read it?
